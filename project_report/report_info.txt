* LECTURERS NOTE

You are expected to be either on your own or form at most 2-3 student groups to investigate a topic
of interest related to nonlinear analysis of structures by using material, geometric nonlinearity or
any form of nonlinearity. You can work on frame, shell or solid finite elements, simulation of RC
or steel structures, aerospace structures, etc... Your research can be theoretical, coding, etc... or
using and describing the advanced nonlinear solution capabilities of available software programs.
You are expected to write a report and submit all the files that you used in the preparation of your
project report.
Write a report that is at least 15 pages long (for single student groups) with 11/12 font size, double
spaced and 2.5 cm margins. If your group contains 2-3 students, then your report should be at least
5 pages more for each additional student. The amount of work should actually theoretically double
with the addition of each student in the group, which should be reflected in increased content
quality.
Your report should have a cover page, table of contents, list of figures and/or tables page, list of
references (not counted in 15 or so pages). Don't exaggerate the size of figures, i.e. the figures
should not cover huge part of the project report.
When you write your report, have an introduction part that summarizes your project topic in
general and then progress with your report's written part. At the end, write a summary and
conclusion part, and possibly some suggestions and recommendations if applicable.
